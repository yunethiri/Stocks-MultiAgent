content: Amazon.com's <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/AMZN.O" target="_blank">(AMZN.O)</a> cloud unit on Tuesday showed new data center servers packed with its own AI chips that will challenge Nvidia <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/NVDA.O" target="_blank">(NVDA.O)</a>, with Apple coming aboard as a customer to use them.
The new servers, based on 64 of Amazon Web Services' Trainium2 chips, will be strung together in a massive supercomputer with hundreds of thousands of chips, with the help AI startup Anthropic, which will be the first to use it. Apple executive Benoit Dupin also said that Apple <a href="https://www.reuters.com/markets/companies/AAPL.O" target="_blank">(AAPL.O)</a> is using Trainium2 chips.
AWS Chief Executive Matt Garman also said that Trainium3, the company's next generation of AI chip, will debut next year.
